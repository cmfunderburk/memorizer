Nash Bargaining Solution

Maximize the Nash Product subject to the feasible set.
(u - d)(v - d') where d is disagreement point.
=== MEMO START ===
Problem:
Max (u(x) - d_u) * (v(y) - d_v)
s.t. (x, y) in Feasible Set S

FOC for efficient bargaining on the frontier v = f(u):
Maximize P = (u - d_u) * (f(u) - d_v)

ln(P) = ln(u - d_u) + ln(f(u) - d_v)

∂ln(P)/∂u = 1/(u - d_u) + f'(u)/(f(u) - d_v) = 0

Rearranging:
-f'(u) = (f(u) - d_v) / (u - d_u)

Interpretation:
Slope of frontier = Slope of hyperbola (level curve of Nash Product).
Or: Loss in v / Gain in u = Share of surplus v / Share of surplus u.
